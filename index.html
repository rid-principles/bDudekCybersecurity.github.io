Obj. 1.2 Notes

C.I.A.N.A and A.A.A:

-Confidentiality: Info is only accessible to those it should be.

-Integrity: Data is considered accurate and correct, hasn't been modified.

-Availability: Is considered "up" and accessible for as much time as possible, can be described by up time.

-Non-repudiation: Something that can prove that something was done and the parties involved cannot claim they were not involved.

-Authentication: Proving you are who you say you are when trying to gain access to resources.

-Authorization: Deciding what a certain user can or cannot do and access.

-Accounting: Properly documenting and taking note of communications from a user during use.

-Gap Analysis: Look at network and determine possible weaknesses or vulnerabilities.

-Zero Trust: A user must provide indentification regardless of where they are logging in from or who they are.
